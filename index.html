How to Measure Bra Size

Follow these 3 simple steps for a perfect fit every time

1

Measure Your Band

Wrap a soft measuring tape snugly under your bust, around your ribcage. Breathe normally. Round to the nearest whole number. This is your under-bust measurement.

2

Measure Your Bust

Measure the fullest part of your chest, keeping the tape horizontal and parallel to the floor. Don't pull too tight. This is your over-bust measurement.

3

Enter & Calculate

Enter both measurements above in our calculator. We'll instantly compute your Indian bra size, cup size, and the sister sizes that work for you.

How We Calculate Your Indian Bra Size

Step 1 — Band Size

Your under-bust measurement gives the band number. If the number is even, that's your band size. If odd, we round to the nearest even number to match Indian sizing standards used by Jockey, Zivame, Clovia, and Triumph India.

Step 2 — Cup Size

Subtract the band size from your over-bust measurement. The difference determines your cup: 0"=AA, 1"=A, 2"=B, 3"=C, 4"=D, 5"=DD/E, 6"=F, 7"=G. Indian sizing follows UK cup standards.

Expert Measurement Tips

For the most accurate results, follow these professional fitting tips

👗

Wear the right bra when measuring

Measure wearing a non-padded, wire-free bra for the most accurate under-bust and over-bust measurements. Avoid sports bras or heavily padded styles.

📐

Keep the tape parallel to the floor

When measuring your bust, ensure the tape is completely horizontal all the way around — especially at the back. An angled tape gives inaccurate readings.

🌬️

Breathe normally while measuring

Don't suck in or push out. Take a natural breath and measure at that relaxed position for the most representative band measurement.

🔄

Re-measure every 6 to 12 months

Your size changes with weight, age, hormones, and life stages. Indian women especially should remeasure after monsoon season weight changes or after festive periods.

🏷️

Always try before buying (brand sizing varies)

Even with your correct size, different Indian brands cut differently. Jockey runs slightly smaller; Triumph India tends larger. When buying online, check brand-specific size charts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about bra sizing in India

How do I measure my bra size at home in India?

+
Use a soft measuring tape. Measure your under-bust (band) by wrapping the tape snugly around your ribcage just below your bust. Then measure your over-bust by wrapping the tape around the fullest part of your chest. Enter both into our calculator above for your instant Indian bra size. Band size = under-bust rounded to nearest even number. Cup size = difference between over-bust and band size.

What is the most common bra size worn by Indian women?

+
The most commonly purchased bra sizes in India are 32B, 34B, and 34C. However, research and studies by Indian lingerie brands suggest that a majority of Indian women are actually wearing incorrect sizes — most commonly wearing bands that are too large and cups that are too small. Getting professionally measured or using an accurate calculator like ours helps find the right fit.

Is Indian bra sizing different from US or UK sizing?

+
Indian bra sizing generally follows the UK sizing system for both band and cup measurements. This means an Indian 34B is equivalent to a UK 34B. US sizing uses different cup letter conventions for larger sizes (US DD = UK E, for example). When shopping on international sites like Amazon or ASOS, always check the size conversion chart. Our calculator uses Indian/UK standards — the same used by Jockey India, Zivame, Clovia, and Enamor.

What are sister sizes in bras and how do I use them?

+
Sister sizes are bra sizes that share the same cup volume but have different band sizes. For example, 34C, 32D, and 36B are all sister sizes — they hold the same amount of breast tissue, just in different band lengths. If your calculated size feels too tight in the band, try the next sister size up (e.g., 36B instead of 34C). If the band is too loose, go to the smaller band sister size (e.g., 32D). Our calculator shows your top sister sizes automatically.

How often should I remeasure my bra size?

+
Experts recommend remeasuring every 6 to 12 months, or whenever you experience significant body changes. This includes weight gain or loss of 5kg or more, pregnancy, postpartum and breastfeeding phases, hormonal changes (including those from medications), and after menopause. Even without obvious changes, elastic in bras stretches over time — so sizing up and refreshing your lingerie collection annually is good practice.

Why does my bra size vary across different Indian brands?

+
Bra sizes aren't perfectly standardized across brands, even within India. Each brand uses slightly different block patterns, fabric stretchability, and cup depth. Jockey India tends to run slightly small in the cup; Triumph India runs true to size; Enamor and Zivame house brands may run slightly large. When shopping online without trying, always check the brand's own size chart and read customer fit reviews. Our calculated size is a reliable starting point — but always check the brand chart too.
